Ham Sandwich Recipe

Ingredients

  • choice of favorite bread (white, sourdough, whole grain)
  • lettuce
  • cheese
  • ham
  • tomato
  • if desired, a condiment of choice (ketchup, mayo, or mustard)

Instructions

  1. Prepare two slices of bread.
  2. If desired, spread condiment or sauce onto one side of each slice of bread.
  3. To replicate Souske's Sandwiches, assemble the ingredients in the following order: lettuce, ham, cheese, and then tomato.
  4. Cut sandwich in half and enjoy!

Steamed Red Bean Rice Buns Recipe

Ingredients

for the red bean paste:

  • 1 cup red beans
  • 3 cups water
  • 3/4 cup granulated sugar
  • 1 tbsp vanilla extract

for the dough:

  • 4 cups flour
  • 2 cups lukewarm water
  • 1 teaspoon granulated sugar
  • 1 tablespoon instant yeast

Instructions

  1. In the bowl of an Instant pot (or similar pressure cooker), wash the beans twice.
  2. Add the water and cook on the program "Beans" for 60 minutes. Make sure the nob on your Instant Pot is pointed towards sealing.
  3. Once done cooking, remove the lid and add the sugar and vanilla extract.
  4. Turn the Instant Pot to saut√© to cook all the water out. This will take anywhere from 30-60 minutes depending on how much water was left in the pot. Make sure to stir constantly so that the beans do not stick to the bottom of the pot. Cook until a paste forms. This step requires lots of patience!
  5. Allow the red bean paste to cool before wrapping the buns.
  6. In the bowl of an electric mixer (or just a large bowl), combine all the ingredients.
  7. Knead on medium for 10 minutes or until a firm slightly elastic dough forms. This dough cannot be sticky or tacky at all. It's better to have a firmer dough than wet. Add flour as needed.
  8. Cover the bowl with a damp cloth and allow to rise for 1 hour or until doubled in size.
  9. Dump dough out onto a floured surface and cut into equal pieces (whatever size you want).
  10. To wrap the buns, flat the dough into a circular disc. Try to make the edges slightly thinner than the center.
  11. Place 1 spoonful of red bean paste into the center. Start to pull the dough up while pushing the filling down with your finger. Crimp the edges all around.
  12. Allow to rest again under a damp towel for 10-20 minutes while your water comes to a boil in your steamer.
  13. Line the steamer with parchment paper and drape a damp cloth over it. Place buns in with about a centimeter of space around each. Steam for 12 minutes.
  14. Turn off the heat and let sit for 1-2 minutes, then remove the lid. Otherwise you risk collapsing the steamed bun with the sudden change in temperature.
  15. Enjoy!

Bento Box Recipe

Ingredients

  • rice
  • umeboshi (pickled plum)
  • edamamde
  • shishamo fish (smelt fish)
  • cod fish (at least one fillet)
  • kombu
  • 2 tbp sake
  • 2 tbsp sugar
  • 1 tsp salt
  • red food coloring

Instructions

  1. Cook the rice.
  2. Shishamo fish (or similar) is usually sold frozen. Defrost and set frying pan to medium heat.
  3. Add some oil and cook shishamo for about 5 minutes until crispy.
  4. Flip over and cook the other side for another 5 minutes.
  5. Place fish on a plate with a paper towel to soak up the extra oil.
  6. Boil water. Once boiled, add the kombu and whole cod. You can tell that the cod is cooked when it can be easily broken into pieces.
  7. Use a paper towel to squeeze out the extra water from the fish. The dryer, the better! On a plate, take out any extra bones.
  8. Prepare a pan or pot on medium-low heat.
  9. Add the sake, sugar, and salt to the pan along with the cod.
  10. Use chopsticks or other utensil that will break the cod into small flakes.Stir well until liquid evaporates. Lower the heat if the cod starts to overcook.
  11. Remove from heat once liquid evaporates.
  12. Add two drops of red food coloring and add a little bit of water (you can make the color light or darker to your choosing).Add the food coloring mixture to the cod. Put under low heat if the flakes get mushy from the water. Mix well until you get your desired pink color.
  13. Boil frozen edamame for 6-10 minutes. Drain water in a colander and shuck the beans.
  14. Assemble the bento box to replicate Satsuki's, or however you prefer and enjoy!
