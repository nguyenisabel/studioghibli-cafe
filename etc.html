Kiki's Chocolate Cake Recipe

Ingredients

for the cake:

  • 1/2 cup granulated sugar (230 g)
  • 1 cup sifted all purpose flour (125 g)
  • 1/4 cup and 2 Tbsp cocoa powder (35 g)
  • 3/4 tsp baking powder (3 g)
  • 3/4 tsp baking soda (3 g)
  • 1/2 tsp salt (2 g)
  • 1 egg
  • 1/2 cup milk (125 ml)
  • 1/4 cup vegetable (or flavorless) oil (50 ml)
  • 1/2 cup just boiled water (125 ml)

for the frosting:

  • 4 Tbsp butter, melted (45 g)
  • 1/3 cup cocoa powder (25 g)
  • 1 1/2 cup icing sugar (185 g)
  • 2-4 Tbsp milk
  • 1/2 tsp. vanilla extract

for the candy melts:

  • 1/2 cup white chocolate (100 g)
  • 1/8 cup green (30 g)
  • 4 tablets red (10 g)

Tips and Tricks

Baking time varies on your oven! So make sure to check on your cake every now and then after the 30 minute mark.

If the top of your cake becomes darker before the rest is cooked, cover it lightly with aluminum foil for the remaining bake time.

Ohagi Recipe

Ingredients

for the anko:

  • 1 cup azuki beans
  • 1 cup sugar (or to taste)
  • pinch of salt

for the rice:

  • 360ml mochi rice
  • 390ml water
  • pinch of salt

optional:

  • kinako powder for topping

Instructions

  1. Soak the beans in water overnight or for 24 hours.
  2. The next day, pour the old water away, and put in new water about 1-2 inches above the beans.
  3. Set beans to boil on high heat.
  4. Reduce to a simmer after it starts to boil. Keep some water handy to add whenever it boils down.
  5. When the beans are soft and you can smush one between your fingers, add the sugar in gradually, stirring with a ladle/slotted spoon.
  6. When you can draw a line at the bottom of the pot with the ladle that lasts for two seconds, it means it's viscous enough and then you can add in the salt, stir, and let it cool.
  7. Set the mochi rice to cook in your rice cooker. (Use the sushi rice function if it has one.)
  8. Sprinkle salt over and then mash it into rough chunks with a wet wooden pestle.

for anko covered ohagi:

  1. Wet a plastic glove or use clingwrap, and spread about 2 tbsp anko on it.
  2. Make a rice ball about 1-1.5 inch in diameter, and place it in the centre of the anko.
  3. Cover all the rice with anko. Add more anko if necessary.
  4. Enjoy!

for kinako covered ohagi:

  1. Spread and flatten the rice on your palm, with a layer of damp plastic in between your hand and the rice. Make it as flat and wide as you can.
  2. Place a dollop of anko in the centre, and work the rice to cover the anko. Add more rice if necessary. (And this was why my kinako ones were bigger than my anko-covered ones)
  3. Pour the kinako (soybean flour) on a plate, and roll the rice-covered ohagi in it.
  4. Enjoy!
